Look up trade marks

Before you apply to register a trade mark for your business, goods or services, you should check if the same or any similar mark is already registered or has been applied for with the Intellectual Property Office.

You can apply to register a trade mark that is identical or similar to an existing one, but you should be aware that the owner of the existing mark will be notified of your application who may decide to oppose your application.

You can search for existing trade marks on the Intellectual Property Office website using the following search options:

  • find an image or text within the trade mark on the Intellectual Property Office website
  • find a trade mark number on the Intellectual Property Office website
  • find the proprietor (an individual or company owning a trade mark) on the Intellectual Property Office website
  • find a trade mark that has been refused registration on the Intellectual Property Office website

You can also use the Intellectual Property Office's classification search on the Intellectual Property Office website. This will enable you to identify the correct classification number(s) of the goods and services your trade mark relates to. You will need this information to complete your application to register a trade mark.

Please note these services may be closed at certain times for maintenance. Please check each search option for availability.
