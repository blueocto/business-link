Approved codes and self regulation

Introduction

There are various reasons why businesses join together and voluntarily regulate their own behaviour, through self-regulation. For example, self-regulatory initiatives are often run by a trade body who ensures its members achieve compliance with consumer law or commits to go beyond what the law requires.

Self-regulation usually involves creating a set of rules, such as a code of practice, voluntary standards or accreditation. Self-regulation can also include arrangements for the provision of industry guidance material and for action to address particular compliance problems.

There are several code of practice schemes which are run or endorsed by Government, such as the Office of Fair Trading's (OFT's) Consumer Codes Approval Scheme - or Approved Code Scheme - TrustMark.

This guide explains the benefits of self-regulation and codes schemes in consumer protection. You will also find information on how your business can get involved with self-regulation and join an approved scheme.
