Mergers

Notifying authorities of a merger

Under the Enterprise Act 2002, there is no requirement to notify a merger to the Office of Fair Trading (OFT), regardless of whether or not the OFT has jurisdiction to review it. For more information on the types of mergers that can be reviewed, see the page in this guide on when does the Office of Fair Trading investigate mergers?

However, many businesses do choose to advise the OFT, typically before the merger occurs, to gain legal certainty. If you decide not to notify the OFT of a merger, this will not affect the OFT's evaluation of it. In fact, in cases where there are clearly no competition concerns (for instance in cases where the activities of the two merging companies do not overlap), you may feel that notifying the OFT would be disproportionate and unnecessary.

Commenting on a merger

When the OFT is considering a merger, it will invite comments from interested third parties - customers, suppliers and/or competitors. Their views may be valuable as they will help define the scope of the market, how competition takes place and what effect the merger may have on competition within any affected market.

If you have any concerns about a merger that affects you, you can raise these directly with the OFT.

You can find details of current merger cases and how to comment on them on the OFT website and find contact details for the OFT for commenting on mergers on the OFT website.

You can also download a merger notice form to notify the OFT of proposed mergers not to be completed before expiry from the OFT website (PDF, 124K) and download guidance on making a prior notice of publicly proposed mergers form from the OFT website (PDF, 99K).
