Mergers

When does the Office of Fair Trading investigate mergers?

When you decide to merge businesses, through takeover, acquisition or a joint venture, you may be reducing competition in your field. If your merger will substantially lessen competition it may be prohibited or you may have conditions imposed upon you.

The Office of Fair Trading (OFT) is responsible for investigating mergers and referring cases to the Competition Commission for an in-depth inquiry if they believe the merger may be expected to result in a substantial lessening of competition.

Relevant merger situations

The OFT only has jurisdiction over 'relevant merger situations' - mergers that meet all three criteria. To qualify, OFT requires that:

  • Two or more enterprises (a business or part of a business) are brought together by the merger.
  • The merger must not have taken place yet, or taken place not longer than four months ago.
  • Either
    • The business being taken over has a turnover in the UK of at least £70 million
    • The combined businesses have significant market presence. This applies if together they supply (or acquire) at least 25 per cent of particular goods or services in the UK (or a substantial part of the UK), and the merger results in an increase in the share of supply or consumption of such goods or services.

Because of this last criterion, many mergers between smaller businesses are unlikely to qualify for investigation.

If you are involved in a merger which does meet these criteria, you may need to seek advice.
