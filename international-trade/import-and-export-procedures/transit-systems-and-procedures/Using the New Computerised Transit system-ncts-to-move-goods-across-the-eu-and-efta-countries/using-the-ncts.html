Using the New Computerised Transit System (NCTS) to move goods across the EU and EFTA countries

Using the NCTS

Once you have successfully opened your account on the New Computerised Transit System (NCTS) you can begin to submit your transit declarations and print the documents that must accompany each of your consignments. This page explains how the basic NCTS features work, and what to do if you require help.

NCTS enables your business to send and receive electronic Transit messages relating to the declarations you submit. To use it, your business must have a compatible computer system and a printer capable of producing Transit Accompanying Documents (TADs), including the barcode.

TADs and List of Items (LoI)

Although NCTS is designed to be a paperless system, your consignments must still travel with a TAD. The TAD includes your consignment's Movement Reference Number - printed in numeric form and as a barcode. You must be able to print barcodes to the ISO code 128 standard, rather than EAN 128.

When more than one item is being consigned, an LoI should also be sent with the TAD.

Transmitting messages on NCTS

You can send and receive messages on NCTS using the web, Electronic Data Interchange For Administration, Commerce and Transport (EDIFACT) or Extensible Mark-up Language (XML) channels.

Transit simplified procedures

Approval as Authorised consignors and consignees are just two of the 'simplified' procedures in transit. If you want to use any of these facilities, contact HMRC Simplifications Team on Tel 01255 244709 or see our guide on transit and other suspensive regimes. You can also download information about all transit simplified procedures from the UK Supplement to the Transit Manual on the HMRC website - Opens in a new window.

De-enrolling from NCTS Web

To find out how to de-enrol a staff member - or your business - from NCTS, read the frequently asked questions on NCTS registration and enrolment on the HMRC website - Opens in a new window.
