Take the approved driving instructor (ADI) part one test: theory

Special needs for the approved driving instructor (ADI) part one test

The Driving Standards Agency (DSA) needs to know if you have special needs. You should tell the DSA about your special needs when you book your theory test.

Facilities if you have reading difficulties

If you have dyslexia or other reading difficulties, you can ask for an English voiceover.

You can also ask for extra time to take the multiple-choice part of the test. If you do need more than the normal time, you must send proof of your reading difficulty to the DSA. The proof could be a letter from:

  • a teacher or other education professional
  • a doctor or other medical professional

If you can't get proof from one of these people, the DSA will consider proof from someone independent who knows about your reading ability, eg an employer.


Where to send the proof of your reading difficulty

You'll need to send the proof to the DSA by writing to:

PO BOX 381
M50 3UW
