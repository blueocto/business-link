Securing loads on vehicles

Loading plans for goods vehicles

The consignor - the person responsible for sending the load - is responsible for ensuring that the vehicle is loaded in such a way that it does not present a danger to other people.

Planning how you secure the load is an important step to keeping staff and other people safe. You should make sure you have the correct equipment on your premises to load vehicles safely.

Preparing a loading plan for each journey can help to raise issues before they become problems. The plan should cover:

  • how the load is to be secured
  • the location and layout of each delivery site, including unloading equipment and facilities

When doing the plan, you should think about:

  • whether the driver will witness the loading
  • who will apply the load restraints, and what they should be
  • how the load will be placed on the trailer bed
  • who will unload the vehicle and what equipment will be required
  • who the driver should report to on arrival
  • what the driver should do if the load shifts during the journey

Ahead of the journey, ask the customers you are delivering to or collecting from for information about facilities and off-loading arrangements on their site.

The Health & Safety Executive (HSE) has published an example loading plan and a guide to delivering safely.
