Fuel consumption, CO2, vehicle tax and things to consider when buying a car

What you can find out with the online Car Fuel Data and cost of vehicle tax service

The Vehicle Certification Agency (VCA) has an online service - Car Fuel Data, that lets you check the official fuel consumption, emissions and cost of vehicle tax figures for all new cars currently on sale in the UK.

It also provides fuel consumption and emissions figures for cars registered on or after 1 March 2001.

Information in the database includes:

  • fuel consumption, CO2 and other exhaust emissions figures for new cars and used cars registered on or after 1 March 2001
  • cost of vehicle tax for cars and other vehicle types

The figures shown are obtained during official tests, which every new model of car must take before it can go on sale in the UK. The database is regularly updated as new vehicles come onto the market.

The official figures are meant to serve as a useful means of comparing the relative economy and CO2 emissions of different vehicle models.

You should, however, bear in mind that the fuel consumption figures quoted are obtained under standard test conditions. This means that it is unlikely that you would achieve these figures under 'real life' driving conditions, where different weather, driving and vehicle conditions might apply.

Use the VCA Car Fuel Data and cost of vehicle tax service to check fuel consumption, emissions and cost of vehicle tax figures for new and some used cars.

Notes about fuel consumption figures

The fuel consumption figures quoted by the Car Fuel Data service are in miles per gallon. Separate figures are given for the 'urban' and 'extra urban' cycles, and a combined figure.

For an explanation of these cycles, see the page in this guide on how car fuel consumption figures are calculated and how to use them.
