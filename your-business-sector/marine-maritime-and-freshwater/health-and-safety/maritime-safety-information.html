Marine safety information, navigational advice and equipment

Maritime Safety Information

HM Coastguard (HMCG) is responsible for broadcasting Maritime Safety Information (MSI) in the UK on VHF, MF and NAVTEX.

The different types of communication have varying ranges of coverage, for example:

  • VHF ranges up to 30 miles
  • MF ranges up to 150 miles
  • NAVTEX ranges up to 270 miles

MSI is broadcast at different frequencies from a network of remote radio sites around the coast of the UK. Typical MSI broadcast frequencies are as follows:

  • VHF channels 23, 84 or 86 and exceptionally channel 10
  • MF broadcasts are made by co-ordination centres on specified frequencies - initial announcements being made on 2182 kilohertz MF, with instructions on where the detailed broadcast will be made

Both MSI and weather information are broadcast on 518 kilohertz for the NAVTEX system.

You should be aware that MSI broadcasts may be interrupted or delayed due to Search and Rescue operations.

You can download MSI and broadcast frequencies information from the MCA website (PDF, 1.40MB) - Opens in a new window.

You can also download Marine Guidance Note (MGN) 375 (M+F) information on MSI on the MCA website (PDF, 1.95MB) - Opens in a new window.

Medical advice and medical assistance

You can use your radio to request medical advice or assistance from the nearest HM Coastguard Maritime Rescue Co-ordination Centres. For further information see the page on medical advice and requests for medical assistance in our guide radio and distress communications at sea.
