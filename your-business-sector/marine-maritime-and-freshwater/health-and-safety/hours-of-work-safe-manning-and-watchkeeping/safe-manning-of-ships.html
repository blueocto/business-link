Hours of work, safe manning and watchkeeping

Safe manning of ships

Under the Merchant Shipping (Safe Manning, Hours of Work and Watchkeeping) Regulations 1997, companies that own or operate UK-registered ships must ensure that their vessels are staffed with properly trained and certificated personnel of appropriate grades. The numbers of certificated officers and certificated and non-certificated ratings must be sufficient to ensure safe and efficient operation of the ship at all times.

For information on training and certification requirements that apply on fishing vessels of 16.5m and over, you can download Marine Guidance Note (MGN) 411 (M+F) Training & Certification Requirements for the Crew of Fishing Vessels & their Applicability to Small Commercial Vessels & Large Yachts from the Maritime and Coastguard Agency (MCA) website (PDF, 389K) - Opens in a new window.

You also need to make sure that everyone working on your vessel has adequate periods of rest and shore leave, which are required under working time regulations. See the page in this guide on working time regulations and safety codes for seafarers.

Dealing with fatigue

Owners and operators are responsible for ensuring that masters and crews are adequately rested to perform their duties safely. Those responsibilities are contained in Marine Guidance Note (MGN) 211 (M). You can download MGN 211 (M) on dealing with fatigue from the MCA website (PDF, 66K) - Opens in a new window and see our guide on improving maritime safety (Human Element). You can also download human behaviour in the shipping industry guidance from the MCA website (PDF, 4.14MB) - Opens in a new window. You can read about the issue of fatigue in the page in this guide on the effects of fatigue on the safety of sea workers.

Safe Manning Documents

All merchant ships over 500 Gross Tonnage on the UK register must have a Safe Manning Document issued by the MCA. Safe Manning Documents:

  • state the numbers and categories of officers and crew members that are required to be on board
  • are only issued after consultation with the owner or manager

Download a Safe Manning Document Application from the MCA website (PDF, 40K) - Opens in a new window. For information on manning, you can download a UK Flag guide for masters from the MCA website (PDF, 5.73MB) - Opens in a new window.

Manning of pilot boats

The code of practice for the safety of small workboats and pilot boats requires that all pilot boats are manned by at least two people. To read about the manning of pilot boats, download MGN 50 (M) - Manning of Pilot Boats from the MCA website (PDF, 160K) - Opens in a new window.

UK Certificates of Equivalent Competency

All officers who do not possess a UK Certificate of Competency must hold a UK Certificate of Equivalent Competency issued by the MCA. For information, see our guide on how to apply for or renew a Certificate of Equivalent Competency.

Human Element Assessment Tools (HEAT)

The MCA has developed two human element assessment tools that you can use free of charge. You can download information on the HEAT from the MCA website (PDF, 132K) - Opens in a new window. You can also read about maritime human element in our guide on improving maritime safety (Human Element).

You can obtain copies of the tools by emailing the MCA Human Element Team at human.element@mcga.gov.uk.
