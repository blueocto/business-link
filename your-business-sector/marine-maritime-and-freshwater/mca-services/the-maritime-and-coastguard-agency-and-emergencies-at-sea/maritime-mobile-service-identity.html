The Maritime and Coastguard Agency and emergencies at sea

Maritime Mobile Service Identity

All modern very high frequency (VHF) and medium frequency (MF) radios are equipped with Digital Selective Calling (DSC). All DSC equipment is programmed with a unique, nine-digit identification number, known as a Maritime Mobile Service Identity (MMSI).

MMSI is a digital selective calling contact number for a vessel. The MMSI is sent automatically with each and every DSC transmission made. A vessel or shore station can use the MMSI number to alert another vessel that they wish to communicate.

The MMSI number is obtained via Ofcom as part of a Maritime Radio Licence application. You can apply for a Maritime Radio Licence on the Ofcom website - Opens in a new window.

Alternatively you can apply by post, using Form Of347, for which there is a fee per licence. Applications should be sent to the following Ofcom address:

Amateur and Maritime Team
Ofcom Licensing Centre
PO Box 56373
London
SE1 9SZ

For help and advice, you can call the Maritime Team Helpline on Tel 0300 123 1000. This helpline is open Monday to Friday from 09.00 to 17.00. You can also use the Maritime Team Textphone on Tel 0300 123 2024 - please note that this number only works with special equipment used by people who are deaf or hard of hearing.

You can also email the licensing centre at licensingcentre@ofcom.org.uk.

DSC acts like a pager and has a short message to indicate which radio channel should be used for any possible radio conversation.

If a distress DSC call is made, any DSC equipped radios that are in range will receive the DSC distress transmission - which is automatically sent as an 'all stations' broadcast.
