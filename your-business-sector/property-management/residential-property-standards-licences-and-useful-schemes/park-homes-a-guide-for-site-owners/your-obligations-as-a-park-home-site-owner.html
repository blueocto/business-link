Park homes: a guide for site owners

Your obligations as a park home site owner

Your obligations, and the rights and obligations of residents, are set out in the implied terms of your written agreement with a resident. For further information on written agreements, see the page in this guide on written agreements for park homes.

A summary of key obligations as park home site owner

As park home site owner, you must give a prospective resident a written agreement, which must include the resident's obligation to pay you a pitch fee and the express and implied terms. Park rules also form an integral part of the agreement if you specifically provide that they are.

You must display a site licence somewhere on site - see the page in this guide on site licences - applications, conditions, enforcement and revocation and recognise and consult any Qualifying Residents Association (QRA) - see the page in this guide on Qualifying Residents Associations.

You must provide accurate written details about the location and size of the pitch if requested by the occupier. You should also provide documentary evidence free of charge to explain any charges such as pitch fee increases or utility costs if requested by the occupier.

You should also repair the base for the park home if necessary, maintain any services supplied to the park home and pitch and keep the site in a clean and tidy condition. You should also consult individual residents on any improvements to the site - particularly if you intend to take them into account at the next pitch fee review.

Express terms in park home written agreements

It is up to you and the resident to agree what express terms should be included in your agreement. Those terms cannot over-rule any of the implied terms and rights contained in the 1983 Act.

You or the resident can ask for any of the express terms to be changed or removed and ask for new terms to be added, provided the new terms are within a list set out in the Mobile Homes Act 1983. The residential property tribunal will then decide what changes, if any, should be made. Otherwise, the agreed express terms will become binding on both sides.

Express terms cannot reduce a resident's statutory or common law rights or impose unfair burdens on them.

It is essential that both parties consider the express terms very carefully. If in any doubt about the effect the terms may have, you should seek advice from:

  • a solicitor
  • a Citizens Advice Bureau
  • the Office of Fair Trading
  • Trading Standards in your local council
  • Consumer Direct

You cannot change the express terms in the agreement if it has been assigned.

Implied terms in park home written agreements

Implied terms set out residents' obligations. There are 28 implied terms in all and they cannot, under any circumstances, be changed. To read them in full, download guidance on implied terms in park home agreements from the Communities and Local Government website (PDF, 107K) - Opens in a new window.

Park home residents' rights and obligations

The implied terms set out residents' obligations. The resident must:

  • pay their pitch fee and any other charges payable under their agreement
  • keep their home in a sound state of repair
  • keep the outside of their home and their pitch clean and tidy
  • if seeking re-imbursement for any costs or expenses, provide documentary evidence of the costs and expenses claimed if the site owner requests it

If park rules form part of the agreement, you should make sure all residents are given a copy.
