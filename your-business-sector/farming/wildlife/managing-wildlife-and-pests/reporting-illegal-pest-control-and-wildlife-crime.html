Managing wildlife and pests

Reporting illegal pest control and wildlife crime

If you suspect that illegal activities are taking place, you should contact your local police force - many of which have specialist wildlife crime officers.

Examples of wildlife crimes include:

  • setting illegal spring traps (eg leghold traps)
  • poisoning protected species
  • cruelty to wildlife
  • illegally trading in protected species

Suspected pesticide poisoning of wildlife

Pesticides should always be used responsibly. The Campaign against Accidental or Illegal Poisoning (CAIP) aims to raise awareness of the issue of poisoning of wildlife and companion animals.

The Chemicals Regulation Directorate is part of the Health & Safety Executive (HSE). They are responsible for the regulation of pesticides and administering the Wildlife Incident Investigation Scheme (WIIS).

You can find information on WIIS on the HSE website - Opens in a new window.

Read advice for farmers about the illegal poisoning of wildlife on the CAIP website - Opens in a new window.

Download CAIP's best practice guide on controlling pests in the countryside from the Agricultural Document Library (ADLib) website (PDF, 2.60MB) - Opens in a new window.

To report a suspected poisoning incident, you can call the WIIS Helpline on Tel 0800 321 600.

You can also get advice about wildlife crime and reporting from the Partnership for Action Against Wildlife Crime (PAW). Find out about reporting wildlife crime on the PAW website - Opens in a new window.

To report a suspected poisoning incident of wildlife or companion animals, you should call the WIIS Helpline on Tel 0800 321 600.
