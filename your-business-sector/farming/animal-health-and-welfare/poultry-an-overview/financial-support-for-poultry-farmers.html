Poultry: an overview

Financial support for poultry farmers

Direct financial support for the poultry industry is limited. However, there are some organisations that provide help and support to poultry farmers.

Poultry and international trade incentives

If you engage in the import or export of poultry or poultrymeat products, the Common Agricultural Policy ensures that European Community producers receive fair prices for these and other goods. This could mean that import licences might be available under various product-based preferential import quota schemes. Similarly, some products may attract an export refund. You can find out about import quotas and export refund schemes for poultry on the Rural Payments Agency website - Opens in a new window.

For more information, you can also read our guide on using trade preferences.

The Rural Development Programme England (RDPE) is jointly funded by the European Union, through the European Agricultural Fund for Rural Development, and the government. You can find details of the full range of RDPE schemes on the Department for Environment, Food and Rural Affairs (Defra) website - Opens in a new window.

There may be opportunities to obtain grants under the agri-environment schemes. These schemes can provide grants towards making environmental improvements to farm holdings. For more details, you can contact the Defra Helpline on Tel 08459 33 55 77.

The Home Grown Cereals Authority operates an Enterprise Awards scheme for users of cereals. This can include cereals used in feed rations or cereal ingredients in value-added production.

Organic poultry farmers may benefit from Organic Entry Level Stewardship (OELS). This voluntary, non-competitive scheme allows you to choose from a wide range of options to cover all farming types. See our guide on Organic Entry Level Stewardship (OELS).

The land to be entered into the scheme must be farmed organically and registered with an approved Organic Inspection Body before an application to OELS is made. Farmers with a mix of organic and conventional land can apply for OELS on their OELS-eligible land and Entry Level Stewardship (ELS) on the remainder - at the applicable ELS payment rates - as part of one, whole farm, OELS agreement. For more information, see our guide on Environmental Stewardship: the basics.

You can also find information on the different types of Environmental Stewardship and OELS on the Natural England website - Opens in a new window.
