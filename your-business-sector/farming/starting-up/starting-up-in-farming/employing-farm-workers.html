Starting up in farming

Employing farm workers

If you are starting up in farming and plan to employ people, you must be aware of the employment regulations specific to this sector.

Wages, conditions and disputes

The Agricultural Wages Board sets minimum pay rates for workers in agriculture, as well as other terms and conditions, such as holidays and rest breaks and these are set out in the annual Agricultural Wages Order. You can obtain a copy of the Order and get further advice on the provisions by phoning the Pay and Work Rights Helpline on Tel 0800 917 2368.

For more information, see our guide on agricultural wages.

Where there is a dispute over pay, the Agricultural Wages Enforcement Team of the Department for Environment, Food and Rural Affairs (Defra) can advise employers.

For more information, see our page on tenant farmers and diversification in our guide on ideas and funding for diversification.

You can also contact Acas to help resolve a dispute. Find out about the work of Acas on the Acas website.

Gangmaster licensing and seasonal labour

If you employ teams of agricultural workers through a sub-contractor, you will need a licence from the Gangmaster Licensing Authority (GLA).

For more information, see our guide on gangmasters licensing.

You may also be eligible to recruit seasonal labour under the Seasonal Agricultural Workers Scheme (SAWS).

Find out about the SAWS on the UK Border Agency website.

Health and safety

Farming sector workers are protected by specific health and safety regulations. These cover areas such as transport, preventing falls, and exposure to chemicals or pathogens.

Find out about health and safety in the agricultural sector on the Health and Safety Executive (HSE) website.

For more information, see our guide on farm health and safety: the basics.
