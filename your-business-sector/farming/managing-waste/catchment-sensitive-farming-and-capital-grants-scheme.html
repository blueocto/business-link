Catchment Sensitive Farming (CSF)

Catchment Sensitive Farming and Capital Grants Scheme

The Catchment Sensitive Farming (CSF) project is a partnership between the Department for Environment, Food and Rural Affairs (Defra), Natural England and the Environment Agency.

CSF seeks to reduce diffuse water pollution from agriculture by encouraging farmers to adopt best practice voluntarily.

CSF operates in priority catchments that cover approximately 45 per cent of the total utilisable agricultural area in England. It is supported by Catchment Sensitive Farming Officers (CSFOs) or through partnerships with local organisations, called catchment partnerships.

Its assistance takes two forms:

  • a Capital Grants Scheme
  • training, information and advice

The 2012/13 Capital Grants Scheme is now closed for applications.  The scheme will be available again in 2013-14 and is targeted to areas within the priority catchments.  A total of £15 million is expected to be available for the scheme in 2013-14.

The scheme aims to help farmers and land mangers within those catchments to tackle diffuse water pollution from agriculture. Grants of up to £10,000 for infrastructure investments such as roofing over manure or slurry stores continue to be awarded on a competitive basis to eligible farmers. Securing an agreement is therefore not guaranteed and acceptance depends on the quality of all applications.

CSF advice and support for farmers

CSFOs operate in priority catchments within England. They are on hand to help farmers tackle the causes of harmful water pollution. There is also support for strategic and catchment partnerships in some further catchments outside of the priority ones.

The Pesticides Voluntary Initiative works with CSF to provide technical and practical support on pesticide best practice in catchments where there are pesticide issues. Download the Voluntary Initiative's information on how you can help to minimise the environmental impact of pesticides from the Agricultural Document Library (ADLib) website (PDF, 498K) - Opens in a new window.

There is also an extensive programme of farmer group events and one-to-one farm visits for:

  • whole farm appraisal
  • soil, nutrient and manure management plans
  • farm infrastructure audits
  • training and advice on slurry handling and storage
  • soil, manure and slurry sampling and analysis
  • machinery demonstrations and spreader calibration

If either individual farms or farmer groups require specialist advice, CSF can deploy specialists from contractors outside of the partnership bodies. Specific contractors are selected as required, determined by their expertise and value for money. You can find Defra's table of contractors eligible to deliver against each category of activity and catchments/river basin districts on the ADLib website - Opens in a new window.
