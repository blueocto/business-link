End-of-life vehicles (ELVs)

Sites that accept waste vehicles

If you operate a business working with end-of-life vehicles (ELVs) you must meet requirements for storing and dismantling ELVs at your site.

Comply with an environmental permit

You must have an environmental permit and comply with its conditions if you dismantle or store waste motor vehicles on your site that have not been depolluted.

Vehicles have been depolluted when they have been treated to the approved standard to remove fluids and other hazardous substances or components.

Comply with storage and treatment standards

If you store and treat ELVs, you must meet environmental standards to be classed as an authorised treatment facility (ATF). These standards include:

  • storing and treating ELVs so they do not harm the environment
  • removing all hazardous liquids and components (depolluting)
  • recycling, storing and disposing of the parts appropriately

You can find ELV guidance for ATFs on the Environment Agency website - Opens in a new window.

Competent operator schemes

If you operate an ATF you will need to demonstrate your technical competence and comply with an approved scheme. For more information on approved schemes, see the page on how to comply with your environmental permit in our guide: environmental permits - who needs one and how to comply.

Follow hazardous waste controls

ELVs that have not been depolluted and some component parts, for example batteries, oil filters and waste engine oil, are hazardous waste. You must:

  • use consignment notes to accompany your hazardous waste, and keep a copy of these for three years
  • keep copies of 'return to producer' forms for three years (these are records of what has happened to your waste)
  • check whether you need to register annually with the Environment Agency

See our guide on managing your hazardous waste.

Register waste exemptions

You must register an exemption from environmental permitting for certain activities.

If you had a waste exemption registered before 6 April 2010, you may need to register a new exemption from environmental permitting or apply for an environmental permit. Download a table of waste exemption transitional arrangements from the Environment Agency website (PDF, 62K) - Opens in a new window.

If you dismantle or store waste motor vehicles that have been depolluted you may need:

  • an environmental permit if you store more than two depolluted waste vehicles
  • a U16 exemption if you store up to two depolluted waste vehicles

Find information on a U16 exemption for using depolluted ELVs for vehicle parts on the Environment Agency website - Opens in a new window.

If you only want to treat scrap metal but not waste vehicles you must register a T9 exemption. Find information on a T9 exemption for the recovery of scrap metal on the Environment Agency website - Opens in a new window.

If you have an exemption from environmental permitting you must comply with the exemption conditions.

For more information on how to apply for an environmental permit or register an exemption, see our guide: environmental permits - who needs one and how to comply.

Good practice

The Environment Agency has prepared a toolkit for ELV ATFs that will help you comply with your environmental permit and manage your environmental performance. Download a copy of the toolkit for authorised ELV treatment facilities from the Environment Agency website (PDF, 952K) - Opens in a new window.
