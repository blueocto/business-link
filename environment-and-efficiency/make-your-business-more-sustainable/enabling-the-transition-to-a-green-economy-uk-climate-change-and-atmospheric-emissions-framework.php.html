Enabling the Transition to a Green Economy - UK climate change and atmospheric emissions framework

Introduction

The government is committed to reducing UK greenhouse gas emissions to at least 80 per cent below 1990 levels by 2050, and by 34 per cent by 2020.

The primary framework to achieve this is the Climate Change Act 2008. This Act sets legally binding emissions reduction targets, using a system of five year 'carbon budgets' set 15 years ahead, to clearly set out the future.

The first four budgets (2008-2027) have been set. Carbon budget four is equivalent to 50 per cent reduction by 2025, subject to European Union (EU) decisions on emissions reduction trajectory under the EU emissions trading system.

The primary framework for tackling climate change in the UK will remain the Climate Change Act 2008, and the government is committed to meeting the carbon budgets. This will require actions by businesses across the whole economy - for example, by reducing industrial emissions and generating low carbon energy - as well as by the government.
