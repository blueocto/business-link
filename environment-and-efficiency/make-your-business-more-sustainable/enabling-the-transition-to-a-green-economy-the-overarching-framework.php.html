Enabling the Transition to a Green Economy - the overarching framework

Introduction

The transition to a green economy forms a key element of the Government's Plan for Growth, which was published alongside the Budget earlier this year, putting the UK on a path to sustainable, long-term economic growth.

This guide explains the overall approach and principles that underpin government policy on the green economy. The government's recent policy documents - eg the draft Carbon Plan, Natural Environment White Paper and Electricity Market Reform White Paper. Future documents, such as the final Carbon Plan and Water White Paper, will also be based on them.

A green economy will mean that businesses and consumers have to behave differently.

There are a number of different policy tools that can be used to help effect change in business. See a figure on the model of behaviour change.

Getting the policy framework right is essential to assist the transition to a green economy while minimising the costs to business. This means outlining, where possible, clear, long-term overall objectives and choosing the right policy mechanisms. It also involves designing policy to work as efficiently as possible and outlining where the benefits justify the costs.
