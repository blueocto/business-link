Enabling the Transition to a Green Economy - unlocking green growth

Introduction

The transition to a green economy depends on the development of greener goods and services. New technologies, changes to production processes and business models will all be required for businesses to make a successful transition. A key to this change is how businesses respond to measures that the government puts in place to help create and drive green markets.

However, some barriers will still remain, such as:

  • infrastructure - currently mainly towards designs based on the use of fossil fuels
  • finance - how to access finance for green investments, such as capital investment in new large infrastructure projects and taking technologies from demonstration to commercialisation
  • skills - employers need to find employees with the required skills
  • innovation - there are risks of uncertainty about the likelihood of success, potential market size and difficulties in capturing the full value of innovation
  • overcoming barriers to growth from higher costs to some sectors

There will therefore continue to be a need for government action to help overcome these barriers and unlock green growth.
