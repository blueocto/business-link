Here's how our hospitality business cut costs by using resources efficiently (Flash video)

Strattons Hotel is a small, family-run hotel employing 19 staff in a secluded country house in the centre of Swaffham, Norfolk. The award-winning hotel opened in 1990 and has ten en-suite double rooms and is developing four eco-lodges. Strattons Hotel has an average occupancy level of 86 per cent and an annual turnover of £600,000.

Here, joint owner Vanessa Scott talks about how they have reduced waste and used resources more efficiently at Strattons Hotel.

Strattons Hotel - Opens in a new window 

Vanessa's top tips:

  • "Separate all waste for either reuse or recycling."
  • "Install refillable pump dispensers in guest rooms to minimise packaging and allow bulk purchase."
  • "Reduce cistern water volumes by using cistern-restriction devices or install dual-flush models."
