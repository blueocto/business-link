Environmental permits - who needs one and how to comply

Permits for listed activities at installations or mobile plant

You must have an environmental permit if your business operates an installation or mobile plant that carries out listed activities. See the page in this guide on applying for an environmental permit.

What are listed activities?

Listed activities include:

  • burning fuel, gasification, liquification and refining activities
  • manufacturing and processing metals
  • manufacturing lime, cement, ceramics or glass
  • manufacturing chemicals, pharmaceuticals or explosives, storing chemicals in bulk
  • incinerating waste, operating landfills, recovering waste
  • using solvents
  • intensive pig and poultry farming

You will be affected if your business carries out a listed activity and it exceeds any threshold limits that apply.

Listed activities are divided into three categories according to their environmental impact and the type of permit they need - Part A(1), Part A(2) and Part B. To check who your regulator is for these activities, see the page in this guide on finding your regulator for environmental permitting.

Part A permits control activities with a range of impacts, including emissions to air, land and water. Part B permits regulate emissions to air (including odour) from specified activities.

For full details of listed activities and mobile plant, you can download guidance on the meaning of a regulated facility from the Environment Agency website (PDF, 656K) - Opens in a new window.

What are installations and mobile plant?

An installation is a stationary technical unit - such as a self-contained building, permanent structure or fixed plant - that is used for one or more listed activities. It also includes any directly associated activities that are carried out at the same site and could have an effect on pollution, such as a treatment plant that is used exclusively to treat waste from a listed activity.

Mobile plant is moveable plant that's used for carrying out specific listed activities or waste operations. Mobile plant that carry out waste operations are called waste mobile plant. For further information on waste mobile plant, see the page in this guide on permits and exemptions for waste activities.

For mobile plant regulated by local authorities to control air pollution, the definition is a little different. It includes plant that is designed to move or be moved whether on roads or other land and that is used to carry on a Part B activity.
