Here's how our business cut resource use by using green chemistry

Harp International Ltd was established in 1991 to provide a complete refrigerant service to the refrigeration and air-conditioning industry. Since then, the business has expanded with the addition of a range of products and services that are principally tailored to the foam-blowing and aerosol-propellant industries.

Harp International is based at Pontypridd in South Wales in a 4,650 square metre plant, which incorporates state-of-the-art packaging and reclamation facilities, together with extensive warehousing and distribution capabilities.

What we did

Harp provides customers with refrigerant gases packaged in steel cylinders, which are used to charge refrigeration systems. When finished with, the cylinders are returned to Harp. They are then emptied of any residual product (which is pooled and reused), internally inspected for faults and repainted prior to refilling.

Offered financial incentives

In an attempt to prevent illegal venting to atmosphere, Harp offered financial incentives to recover waste refrigerant. In return, Harp can reprocess and resell the refrigerant, thus generating a secondary revenue stream. Waste refrigerants returned to Harp for reprocessing and reuse are put in special reclaim cylinders which are distinguished from the pure product cylinders by a yellow band.

Reduced solvent use

The original paint plant, designed for solvent-based paints, was installed in 1992. Although the annual consumption of solvents fell short of being regulated by statutory requirements, a business decision was made to upgrade the facility and convert to using less hazardous, water-based paints. Aside from the potential to achieve greater plant efficiency, the main driver leading to this move was to improve the business' overall performance in line with the objectives of its environmental policy.

Although water-based paints are more expensive, the increased costs have been offset by the plant operating much more efficiently. The substitution has reduced solvent emissions by some 30 per cent and much less hazardous waste is generated. In addition, staff no longer handle hazardous substances.

Automated stencilling

After painting, each cylinder is stencilled with the gas type and net weight of product. Previously, this process was carried out manually using aerosol paint cans. An inkjet stenciller was installed and the cylinders now pass through a programmable printing head. Just one litre of ink is used to stencil one year's supply of cylinders, compared with about 400 aerosol cans. Emissions are significantly reduced and annual material costs reduced from £788 to £138. The disposal of 398 used aerosol cans as hazardous waste was also eliminated.

What the benefits were

The benefits of green chemistry to Harp International included:

  • costs reduced by over £2,000 per year
  • hazardous, solvent-based paints replaced with a less hazardous alternative
  • increased process efficiency
  • reduced exposure of employees to hazardous substances
  • lower solvent emissions from the site
  • lower quantities of hazardous waste generated on site

Harp International's top tips:

  • Look to recover residual product which can be reprocessed and reused.
  • Use water-based paints, which are less hazardous.
  • Switch from using aerosol cans to an inkjet stencil to reduce volatile organic compound emissions and eliminate hazardous waste production.
