Reuse of a company name after liquidation

Introduction

If you are a former director of a liquidated company, there are legal restrictions that apply regarding the reuse of that company's name or its trading name. This is intended to prevent abuse of the so-called 'phoenix company' - where a failed business re-emerges to operate under a similar name.

A name which cannot be used is known as a 'prohibited name', and its use is restricted in certain circumstances. The restrictions apply personally to you and anyone else who was a director - or acted as a director - at any time during the 12 months leading up to the company's liquidation.

This guide explains what counts as a prohibited name, the restrictions involved and the penalties you could face for breaking the rules. It also has information about exceptions to the rules which make it possible to legally reuse a prohibited name.

This guide is intended to be an introduction to the topic. To understand exactly how these restrictions affect you, you should always get independent, professional advice.
