Sanctions on behaviour in event of a bankruptcy

Introduction

If you are made bankrupt and the official receiver (OR) considers that your conduct has been dishonest or blameworthy in some way, they will report the facts to the court and ask for a bankruptcy restrictions order (BRO) to be made.

The OR is a civil servant in The Insolvency Service and an officer of the court. The court will consider this report and any other evidence put before it, and will decide whether it should make a BRO. If it does, you will be subject to certain restrictions for the period stated in the order, which can be from two to 15 years.

This guide details how the OR applies for a BRO, and why they might decide to do so. It also explains the option to enter into a Bankruptcy Restrictions Undertaking as an alternative to the BRO, and what will happen if your bankruptcy is annulled (cancelled).
