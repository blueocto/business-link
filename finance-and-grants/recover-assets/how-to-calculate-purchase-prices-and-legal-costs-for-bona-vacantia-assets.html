How to calculate purchase prices and legal costs for bona vacantia assets

Introduction

When a registered company is dissolved before its members have transferred its assets out of the company, all of its property and rights pass to the Crown as ownerless property or 'bona vacantia'. These assets are then dealt with by the Treasury Solicitor. This is not the case for a dissolved company's liabilities.

This guide explains how the purchase prices for bona vacantia are calculated for the different types of property and rights you might be interested in obtaining, and gives example calculations. It also details what the Treasury Solicitor's fixed charges and disbursements will be for its work on the sale.

This guide contains detailed guidance on costs intended for those wishing to apply for bona vacantia grants or rights. For basic guidance on dissolved company assets, bona vacantia and the role of Treasury Solicitor, see our guide on dissolved company assets: the basics.
