What happens to assets owned by a dissolved company

Introduction

When a company registered under the Companies Act is dissolved, all its property and rights in England and Wales (but not its liabilities) pass to the Crown as ownerless property or 'bona vacantia'. If the company's last registered office was in England or Wales - other than in the Duchies of Cornwall or Lancaster - the Treasury Solicitor is nominated by the Crown to deal with its property.

This guide describes what happens to assets once they pass to the Crown as bona vacantia, how the Treasury Solicitor deals with the different types of asset and liability, and what you may be able to claim as a result. For basic information on dissolved companies, the role of the Treasury Solicitor, and bona vacantia, see our guide on dissolved company assets: the basics.
