Shares owned by a dissolved company

Introduction

When a company is dissolved, the Treasury Solicitor can sell shares that have passed to the Crown as ownerless property or 'bona vacantia'. This guide explains how to give the Treasury Solicitor the information they need in order to sell the shares, what the Treasury Solicitor can and can't do with the shares and the sales process.

The guide aims to help people who want to refer bona vacantia shares to the Treasury Solicitor. This may be because you have been affected by a company closure, or simply because you wish to buy shares that were previously owned by a dissolved company.

For basic guidance on dissolved company assets, bona vacantia, and the role of Treasury Solicitor see our guide dissolved company assets: the basics.
