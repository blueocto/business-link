Repaying funds to restored companies

Introduction

If a registered company is dissolved, the assets pass to the Crown as ownerless property or 'bona vacantia'.

Usually the Treasury Solicitor is then responsible for dealing with the company's property and rights. However, if you are a former member or creditor of a dissolved company, you may be able to restore the company to the register, at which time the assets will re-vest in the company.

This guide aims to help former company members and creditors apply to the Treasury Solicitor for the repayment of the company's monies held by them.

For basic guidance on dissolved company assets, bona vacantia and the role of Treasury Solicitor, see our guide on dissolved company assets: the basics.
