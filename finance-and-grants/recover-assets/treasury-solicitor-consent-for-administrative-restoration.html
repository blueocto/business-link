Treasury Solicitor consent for administrative restoration

Introduction

If a company has been dissolved, it is illegal to continue trading under that name. However, if the members of a dissolved company want to regain possession of the company's property and rights, it is sometimes possible to restore the company to the Companies Register. However, in order to restore a company, you will first need the Treasury Solicitor's permission.

This guide gives you information about requesting Treasury Solicitor consent or a waiver letter for the restoration of a dissolved company to the Companies Register.

You can also find guidance on when a company is eligible for restoration, and how to apply using the BVC14 application form. There is also information on the costs of applying, how long the process usually takes and how to use this consent to apply to Companies House to restore a company.

Please note, this guide contains detailed guidance. For basic guidance on dissolved company assets, bona vacantia, and the role of Treasury Solicitor see our guide on dissolved company assets: the basics.
