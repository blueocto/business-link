The distribution of a dissolved company's share capital

Introduction

When a registered company is dissolved before its members have transferred its assets out of the company, all of its property and rights in England and Wales - but not its liabilities - pass to the Crown as ownerless property or 'bona vacantia'. It is then dealt with by the Treasury Solicitor.

Usually, the property and rights will be disclaimed - ie the Crown gives up its right to the property - or sold, and the proceeds of sale transferred to the Exchequer to be dealt with in the same way as money raised by general taxation.

This guide explains how company shares are dealt with when a company is dissolved, and how to maintain share capital and distribute a company's share capital to its members prior to its dissolution. It also provides information on the share capital distribution limit and the types of distribution which are of interest to the Treasury Solicitor.

For basic guidance on dissolved company assets, bona vacantia, and the role of Treasury Solicitor see our guide on dissolved company assets: the basics.
