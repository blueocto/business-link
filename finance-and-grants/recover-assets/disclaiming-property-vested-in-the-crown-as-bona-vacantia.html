Disclaiming property vested in the Crown as bona vacantia

Introduction

When a company is dissolved, its assets pass to the control of the Treasury Solicitor. They then have the right to sell such properties.

In some situations - eg when the property is in negative equity - the Treasury Solicitor may give up the Crown's right to deal with the property, ie 'disclaim' it. The Treasury Solicitor makes such decisions on a case-by-case basis.

This guide gives detailed information about the Treasury Solicitor's power to disclaim properties. It explains how you can apply to have a property disclaimed and what happens to disclaimed properties. It also explains how the Treasury Solicitor approaches property and rights that pass to the Crown as bona vacantia and the information you should provide to the Treasury Solicitor.

For basic guidance on dissolved company assets, bona vacantia and the role of the Treasury Solicitor, see our guide on dissolved company assets: the basics.
