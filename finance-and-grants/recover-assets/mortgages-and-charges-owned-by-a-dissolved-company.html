Mortgages and charges owned by a dissolved company

Introduction

When a company is dissolved, all of its property passes to the Crown as ownerless property or 'bona vacantia'. It is then dealt with by the Treasury Solicitor's department.

The Crown will normally give up or 'disclaim' its right to the property. Alternatively, the assets are sold and the proceeds of sale transferred to the Exchequer.

This guide contains detailed information on what to do if your property is subject to a mortgage or charge in favour of a company that has been dissolved. In this situation, the benefit of the debt due under the mortgage or other debt will pass to the Crown. This applies to money that was borrowed from the company and secured by a mortgage over your property. Other debts - known as 'charges' - that were owed to the company and secured over your property will also be passed to the Crown.

For basic guidance on dissolved company assets, bona vacantia, and the role of Treasury Solicitor, see our guide on dissolved company assets: the basics.
