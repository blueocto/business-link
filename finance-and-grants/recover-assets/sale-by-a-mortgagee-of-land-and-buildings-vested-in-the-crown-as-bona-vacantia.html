Sale by a mortgagee of land and buildings vested in the Crown as bona vacantia

Introduction

When a company that was registered under the Companies Act is dissolved, all its property passes to the Treasury Solicitor as ownerless property or 'bona vacantia'. This is not the case for the dissolved company's liabilities.

This guide gives you information about the sale by a mortgagee of land and buildings that are vested in the Crown as bona vacantia. It also explains the role of the Treasury Solicitor, the steps to take when preparing for and proceeding with a sale, and what documents and information about your property you should provide to the Treasury Solicitor in the event of a sale.

For basic guidance on dissolved company assets, bona vacantia, and the role of Treasury Solicitor see our guide on dissolved company assets: the basics.
