Discretionary grants for dissolved companies that cannot be restored

Introduction

When a company is dissolved, all its property - but not its liabilities - pass to the Treasury Solicitor as ownerless property or 'bona vacantia'. The Crown can then give part or all of it away by a grant from the Treasury Solicitor. This power is discretionary.

This guide gives you information about the Treasury Solicitor's role in grants from bona vacantia. It tells you how to apply for a discretionary grant where it is not possible to restore a company, what proof of identity and documents you will need and additional information to help you with your application.

This guide only applies if the company cannot be restored to the Companies Register. For companies that can be restored to the Companies Register, see our guide on discretionary grants for dissolved companies that can be restored.

Please note, this guide contains detailed guidance for those who wish to apply for a discretionary grant. For basic guidance on dissolved company assets, bona vacantia and the role of Treasury Solicitor, see our guide on dissolved company assets: the basics.
