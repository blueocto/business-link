Intellectual property and rights owned by a dissolved company

Introduction

Following a registered company's dissolution, the Treasury Solicitor can sell any of its intellectual property (IP) and rights that have passed to the Crown as ownerless property or 'bona vacantia'.

This guide aims to help people who want to refer ownerless IP and rights to the Treasury Solicitor. This may be because you have been affected by a company closure or because you wish to buy IP and rights that were previously owned by a dissolved company.

This guide explains the process the Treasury Solicitor follows in order to sell IP and rights. It outlines what the Treasury Solicitor can and can't do with intellectual property and rights, except for trademarks and copyright. For more information, see our guide on trade marks owned by a dissolved company.

This guide contains general advice on the sale of IP and rights. For basic guidance on dissolved company assets, bona vacantia and the role of Treasury Solicitor, see our guide on dissolved company assets: the basics.
