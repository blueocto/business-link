Adverse possession of land and buildings where the Title is registered

Introduction

When a company that was registered under the Companies Act is dissolved, all its property pass to the Treasury Solicitor as ownerless property or 'bona vacantia'. This is not the case for the dissolved company's liabilities.

This guide gives you information about claiming adverse possession to land and buildings that have passed to the Crown as bona vacantia. It explains what the Treasury Solicitor can and can't do for you, and what you will need to do when the property's title is registered.

This guide only applies to land and buildings with a registered title. In the case of land without a registered title, see our guide on adverse possession of land and buildings where the Title is not registered.

For basic guidance on dissolved company assets, bona vacantia and the role of Treasury Solicitor, see our guide on dissolved company assets: the basics.
