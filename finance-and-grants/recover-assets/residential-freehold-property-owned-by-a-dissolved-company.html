Residential freehold property owned by a dissolved company

Introduction

When a company is dissolved, its assets - including any residential freehold property - pass to the control of the Treasury Solicitor. They then have the right to sell such properties.

If you are a leaseholder in a property formerly owned by a dissolved company, you will have the opportunity to buy the freehold of your building.

This guide explains how leaseholders can apply to the Treasury Solicitor to buy the freehold of their property, and the documentation and conditions involved. For basic guidance on dissolved company assets, bona vacantia, and the role of Treasury Solicitor, see our guide on dissolved company assets: the basics.
