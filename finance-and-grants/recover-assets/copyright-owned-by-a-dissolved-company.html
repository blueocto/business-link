Copyright owned by a dissolved company

Introduction

This guide gives information about the sale by the Treasury Solicitor of copyright formerly belonging to a dissolved company.

It explains when copyright can be sold, the information you need to give the Treasury Solicitor to purchase copyright, how copyright can be withdrawn from sale if a dissolved company is restored to the Companies Register, and how the Treasury solicitor can give up its rights to the copyright of dissolved companies.

Please note that this guide contains detailed guidance intended for those who wish to apply to purchase copyright from the Treasury Solicitor. For basic guidance on dissolved company assets, bona vacantia, and the role of Treasury Solicitor, see our guide on dissolved company assets: the basics.
