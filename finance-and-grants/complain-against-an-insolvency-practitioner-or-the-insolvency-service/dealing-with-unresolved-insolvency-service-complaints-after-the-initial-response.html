Complain against an insolvency practitioner or The Insolvency Service

Dealing with unresolved Insolvency Service complaints after the initial response

If you have informed The Insolvency Service that you are dissatisfied with its service and with the initial response to your complaint, you should write to the relevant director or section head.

You should have been given their details - from the previous complaint - but if not you can find the contact details by calling the Insolvency Service Enquiry Line on Tel 0845 602 9848 or emailing them at insolvency.enquiryline@insolvency.gsi.gov.uk.

What if my complaint has still not been resolved?

If you remain dissatisfied with the response you can ask the Adjudicator to look into your complaint. The Adjudicator is an unbiased referee who makes independent recommendations. You can contact the Adjudicator at:

The Adjudicator's Office
8th Floor
Euston Tower
286 Euston Road
London
NW1 3US

You can also call the Adjudicator's Office Enquiry Line on Tel 0300 057 1111.

For more information about the role of the Adjudicator's Office, you can ask for booklet AO6 - The Adjudicator's Office for complaints about The Insolvency Service.

You can also download booklet AO6 from the Adjudicator's Office website (PDF, 1.63MB).

The Adjudicator will be unable to assist if your complaint arises following a request for information under either the Freedom of Information Act 2000 or a subject access request for personal information under the Data Protection Act 1998. For more information on data protection, see our guide on how to comply with data protection legislation.

If you remain dissatisfied with the way The Insolvency Service has handled your request for information, you can write to the Information Commissioner at:

Wycliffe House
Water Lane
Wilmslow
Cheshire
SK9 5AF

You can also call the Information Commissioner's Office on Tel 01625 545 745.

Find information on the role of the Information Commissioner on the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) website.

The Information Commissioner might not consider your request until you have completed the Insolvency Service's complaints procedure.
