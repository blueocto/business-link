Directory of authorised professional bodies

There are a number of professional bodies recognised by the Secretary of State to authorise insolvency practitioners (IPs).

The Institute of Chartered Accountants in England and Wales (ICAEW) provides members with ethical and technical standards information to ensure high standards of business and public interest. You can write to ICAEW at:

Professional Standards Office
Metropolitan House
321 Avebury Boulevard
Milton Keynes
MK9 2FZ

Read about the ICAEW on the ICAEW website.

The Insolvency Practitioners Association (IPA) is a membership body for those in insolvency practice who promote and maintain performance standards and professional conduct levels for those involved in the insolvency sector. You can write to IPA at:

Valiant House
4-10 Heneage Lane
London
EC3A 5DQ

Read about the work and services of the IPA on the IPA website.

The Institute of Chartered Accountants of Scotland (ICAS) is a professional body of accountants that oversees the professional conduct of accountants in Scotland. You can write to ICAS at:

CA House
21 Haymarket Yards
Edinburgh
EH12 5BH

You can also call the ICAS Helpline on Tel 0131 347 0100.

Find information on the work and services of ICAS on the ICAS website.

Chartered Accountants Ireland is a professional body of accountants that oversees the professional conduct of accountants in Ireland. You can write to them at:

The Linenhall
32-38 Linenhall Street
Belfast
BT2 8BG

Find information about Chartered Accountants Ireland on the Chartered Accountants Ireland website.

Complaints about solicitors are handled by the Legal Ombudsman. They provide a free and independent service for people dissatisfied with their representatives' services. You can write to them at:

Legal Ombudsman
PO Box 15870
Birmingham
B30 9EB

Alternatively, you can call the Legal Ombudsman on Tel 0300 555 0333.

Find out about the Legal Ombudsman on the Legal Ombudsman website.

The Law Society of Scotland investigates complaints about solicitors in Scotland. You can write to them at:

The Law Society's Hall
26 Drumsheugh Gardens
Edinburgh
EH3 7YR

Alternatively, you can call the Law Society of Scotland on Tel 0131 226 7411.

The Insolvency Practices Council (IPC) keeps the appropriateness of the ethical and professional standards of IPs under review, with regard to the public interest in insolvency regulation.

The IPC is not an Ombudsman, and is not in a position to investigate individual cases. You can write to IPC at:

The Secretary
Insolvency Practices Council
PO Box 698
Godalming
GU7 9AR

Alternatively, you can call the IPC Helpline on Tel 01483 424 311 or email them at secretary@insolvencypractices.org.uk.
