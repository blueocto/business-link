Complain against an insolvency practitioner or The Insolvency Service

How The Insolvency Service responds to complaints

If The Insolvency Service agrees with your complaint and admits to the error, you can expect any - or a combination - of the following written statements:

  • an apology
  • an explanation
  • assurance that the error will not happen again
  • details of actions taken to put things right

In certain circumstances, you may also be eligible for financial compensation.

Claiming back your costs

If The Insolvency Service makes a mistake or causes an unreasonable delay, you may be entitled to claim back the costs you have incurred as a direct result. The compensation due will depend on the:

  • nature of the mistake
  • circumstances in which the mistake was made
  • actual loss or costs you incur as a direct result of the mistake

Each claim is considered on its own merits.

Compensation for worry and distress caused directly by The Insolvency Service

If the actions of The Insolvency Service have affected you - eg through stress or worry - you should contact them. In some instances, they may be able to pay compensation to acknowledge and apologise for your treatment. These payments usually range from £25 to £250.

Further mistakes or delays in dealing with your complaint

If your complaint is handled poorly, or The Insolvency Service takes an unreasonable time to deal with it, they may pay you compensation in addition to any reasonable costs. These payments usually range from £25 to £250.

Other complaints The Insolvency Service can help with

As an authorising body for insolvency practitioners (IPs), The Insolvency Service will assist you with complaints about IPs on its records. To find out how it can assist and the information you need to include, see the page in this guide on how to make a complaint about an authorised insolvency practitioner.
