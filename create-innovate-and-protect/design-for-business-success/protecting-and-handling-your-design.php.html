Protecting and handling your design

Introduction

If you create a new product which has a unique appearance, you automatically have design right protection, which lasts for up to 15 years.

Design registration legally protects the overall visual appearance of a product in the geographical area that you register it. It relates to what something looks like - ie the appearance of a product through its shape, visual qualities, lines, contours, colours, shape, texture, materials or pattern. A registered design can be a valuable asset, and can extend design right protection to other countries.

Unregistered Design Right automatically protects the 3D shape and configuration of your designs in the UK and prevents others from copying or misusing them. However, it does not give you protection for two-dimensional aspects such as patterns.

This guide shows you how to manage your designs and make changes to them. It provides guidance on how to enforce your design protection, defend against design infringements and resolve disputes. You can also find out how to buy design rights, object to design registrations, protect your design abroad and how to respect design rights.

This guide applies specifically to design rights in the UK. Within the European Union (EU), designs can be protected through Registered Community Designs (RCDs) and Community Design Right, which is unregistered. These cover the whole of the EU, and are therefore relevant to UK businesses.
