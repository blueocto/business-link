Autumn Statement 2011

How the Autumn Statement will affect your business

On 6 December, the Government published draft Finance Bill 2012 clauses and other tax updates. A full list of all of the documents published by both HM Revenue and Customs and HM Treasury are available on the HMRC website - Opens in a new window.

The Chancellor of the Exchequer delivered his 2011 Autumn Statement to Parliament at 12:30pm on 29 November 2011.

The Autumn Statement provides an update on the Government's plans for the economy based on the latest forecasts from the Office for Budget Responsibility - Opens in a new window.

You can find the Autumn Statement document in full here - Opens in a new window (PDF, 3MB).

Key measures in the Autumn Statement for businesses include:

Credit for smaller businesses 

The Government has announced up to £21bn of support (known as credit easing) through a National Loan Guarantee Scheme, which will lower the cost of bank loans for businesses with turnover up to £50 million, and a Business Finance Partnership to provide a non-bank investment souce for mid-sized businesses and SMEs.

In addition the Government will extend the Enterprise Finance Guarantee (EFG) from January 2012 to include businesses with up to £44 million annual turnover, and a number of new lenders will be accredited to offer EFG lending.

Infrastructure investment and growth

The Government has updated its National Infrastructure Plan - Opens in a new window and announced measures to support growth. These include:

  • introducing a new build indemnity scheme for builders and lenders to stimulate the construction of new homes, and launching a new £400 million Get Britain Building investment fund;
  • launching a new Seed Enterprise Investment Scheme (SEIS) from April 2012, offering 50% income tax relief on investments, and a capital gains tax exemption on gains realised in 2012–13 and then invested through SEIS in the same year;
  • making 100% capital allowances available in the Enterprise Zones in Sheffield, the Black Country, Liverpool, Tees Valley, North Eastern, and the Humber; introducing an 'above the line' tax credit in 2013 to encourage research and development activity by larger companies;
  • announcing an increase in the qualification period for unfair dismissal claims from one to two years from April 2012, as well as consulting or calling for evidence on other measures to reduce the burdens of employment law; and
  • doubling to 50,000 the number of SMEs supported each year by UK Trade and Investment (UKTI) and spend £10 million to provide 500 mid-sized businesses each year with UKTI's bespoke export service.

Fuel duty

The Government has deferred the 3.02 pence per litre fuel duty increase due to take effect on 1 January 2012 to 1 August 2012; the second increase planned for 1 August 2012 will be cancelled.

Business rates

At Budget 2011, the Small Business Rate relief holiday was extended by one year to October 2012. This has now been extended again until April 2013. In addition, all businesses will be allowed to defer 60% of the increase in next year's business rates to the two following years.

Further information and support

For further information on all of the announcements in the Autumn Statement you can visit the HM Treasury website - Opens in a new window, BIS website - Opens in a new window and Directgov website - Opens in a new window.
