Web Fuelled Business

SMEs are changing the way they do business

Greater and more effective use of the web and associated technologies can help small business owners and managers realise growth and efficiency.  Small and medium-sized enterprises (SMEs) need to adopt a strategy that incorporates strong web-based approaches in their business model that will better place them to meet the global competitive challenge, increase competitive advantage and thus enhance profitability and job creation.

Adopting a web-based approach is more than just selling online. It's about such things as driving marketing through social media, increasing website exposure through search engine optimisation and procuring and enhancing supply chains through business-to-business websites. Web Fuelled Business provides tools helping small firms achieve global and local reach and increased productivity.
 
Doug Richards (a former Dragon from Dragon's Den), with support from the Department for Business Innovation and Skills (BIS), has created a series of free videos which will teach you how to fully leverage the Internet.
 
You will learn how to create an identity across the web, build a brand through social media and search engine marketing. You will also learn how to find customers through pay per click and viral other techniques, how to sell online effectively and build a website that has an impact on your customers.
