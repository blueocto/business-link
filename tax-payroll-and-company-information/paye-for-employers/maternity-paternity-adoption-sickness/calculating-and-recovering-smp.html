How to calculate and recover Statutory Maternity Pay

Calculating and recovering SMP

If you don't have payroll software which includes Statutory Maternity Pay (SMP) calculations, there are other options you can use to calculate SMP. The sections below explain each of these ways.

It's easier and quicker to choose a calculator as an option than to do it manually as both calculators will also work out if you are entitled to recover any SMP you pay.

Calculating and recovering using an online calculator

The online calculator is a simple interactive calculator available from the link below.

The SMP calculator works out how much SMP you'll have to pay and how much you can recover. It's easy to use and does everything for you.

The calculator will work out:

  • whether your employee is entitled to be paid SMP
  • the amount of SMP you should pay and from what date
  • how much you can recover

You can review your answers and save them to come back to at a later date. Once you have completed the tool your answers and the results can be printed off so they're ready to enter in whatever payroll system you are using.

The calculator will not cover:

  • babies born earlier than the 15th week before their due date
  • babies born or due on or after 17 July 2011

Calculate Statutory Maternity Pay and recovery

Calculating before 17 July 2011

Use the SMP calculator which is part of HMRC's Basic PAYE Tools package. If you don't already use Basic PAYE Tools you need to download the whole software in order to use the SMP calculator.

The SMP calculator works out how much SMP you'll have to pay and how much you can recover. It's easy to use and does everything for you.

The calculator will work out:

  • whether your employee is entitled to be paid SMP
  • the amount of SMP you should pay and from what date
  • how much you can recover

You can print the figures worked out by the SMP calculator so they're ready to enter in:

  • the P11 Calculator that comes as part of HMRC's Basic PAYE Tools package
  • your commercial software (but this may have its own SMP calculator)
  • a paper form P11

The calculator will not cover:

  • if the baby was born before the intended start date of the maternity pay period
  • if the employee was absent due to a pregnancy related illness and the absence is within four weeks of the week before the baby is due

Interactive Learning Zone material can be found in HMRC's SMP Learning Zone. This includes scenarios which can be used to practice using the calculator. You can access the Learning Zone from the Basic PAYE Tools package.
