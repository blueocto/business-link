Completing your tax return (partners and partnerships)

Introduction

If your business is run as a partnership, as a partner you'll have to complete a Self Assessment tax return (SA100) and a partnership supplementary page (SA104S or SA104F). On it you'll show your income from the partnership, as well as other personal income, gains and expenses.

The nominated partner must also complete a Partnership Tax Return (SA800), this will show each partner's share of the profits or losses.

All partners are jointly liable for any penalties and interest if the Partnership Tax Return is late or inaccurate.

Before they complete the first return, the nominated partner needs to register the partnership for business taxes with HM Revenue & Customs (HMRC). Each partner needs to register for Self Assessment too.

You can send returns online or on paper. If you send them online, you'll have three months longer to complete and send them. You'll need to buy commercial software to send the Partnership Tax Return online.
