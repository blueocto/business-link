Aggregates Levy

Aggregates Levy exemptions

Certain materials - such as soil - are exempt from Aggregates Levy altogether, while some activities may also be exempt from the levy.

However, if you are exploiting a mixture of exempt and non-exempt materials, you still need to record the weight of exempt aggregates and report them on your Aggregates Levy Return.

Materials exempt from Aggregates Levy

Some substances - or materials including a certain percentage of those substances - may be exempt from the levy or outside its scope, depending on the specific circumstances. These include:

  • coal, lignite, shale, slate, clay
  • soil or vegetable or other organic matter
  • industrial minerals (such as metal ores, gypsum, fluorspar) and their processing waste - but not their extraction waste
  • cut building stone and dimension stone - but not offcuts
  • limestone for the production of lime or cement
  • colliery spoil
  • waste from industrial combustion processes or the smelting or refining of metals
  • drill cuttings from oil exploration from the seabed
  • china clay and ball clay waste
  • aggregate arising from utility trenching, the site of a building (footprint only), highways excavation, navigational dredging, or railway construction
  • aggregate that has previously been used for construction

However, the overburden from the extraction of china clay, ball clay, coal, lignite, slate, shale and any industrial minerals is liable for the levy unless it is itself a specifically exempt material.

Read information on Aggregates Levy and exempt materials in Notice AGL1 on the HM Revenue & Customs (HMRC) website - Opens in a new window.

Activities that are exempt from Aggregates Levy

Even if you quarry or otherwise exploit materials that are liable to the levy, certain activities are exempt - such as movement between sites covered by the same registration.

Read information on activities exempt from Aggregates Levy in Notice AGL1 on the HMRC website - Opens in a new window.
