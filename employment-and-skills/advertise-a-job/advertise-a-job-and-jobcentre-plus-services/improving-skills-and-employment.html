Advertise a job and Jobcentre Plus Services

Improving skills and employment

Businesses like yours can help get Britain working by creating opportunities for people looking for work. You can make a real difference to your local community by helping develop skills and creating jobs.

You could support:

Wage Incentives

You may be able to claim a wage incentive of up to £2,275 if you recruit an 18-24 year old a job through the Work Programme. Wage incentives are also available through Jobcentre Plus if you take on a young person living in one of 20 local authority areas. Find out which areas wage incentives are available through Jobcentre Plus - Opens in a new window.

You can read more about the wage incentive scheme on the DWP website - Opens in a new window.

Work Clubs

Work Clubs provide unemployed people with a place to meet and exchange skills, find opportunities, share experiences and receive support to help them in their return to work. Download information and details of who to contact in the Could you run a Work Club leaflet from the Department for Work and Pensions(DWP) website (PDF, 417K) - Opens in a new window.

Work Experience

Work Experience offers young unemployed people the chance to gain valuable work experience. You could benefit from the fresh perspective that a young jobseeker could bring to your business and you would be helping your local community by giving someone a greater chance of finding work. Download the 'Could you offer Work Experience?' guide from the DWP website (PDF, 463K) - Opens in a new window.

Work Together

Work Together encourages unemployed people to take up volunteering as a way of developing skills and improving their employment prospects while they are looking for work. Many local organisations, including the voluntary and community sector, social enterprises and businesses can play a key role in supporting the delivery of Work Together. Download the Work Together leaflet from the DWP website (PDF, 50K) - Opens in a new window.

Enterprise Clubs

These are set up, and run by local businesses and partnerships, for people who are out of work and are thinking about starting their own business or becoming self-employed. Download the 'Could you run an Enterprise Club?' guide from the DWP website (PDF, 228K) - Opens in a new window.

Sector-based work academies

These provide accredited, sector-based pre-employment training and work experience for unemployed people. Jobcentre Plus wants to work with you and local partners to develop training and work experience opportunities leading to a guaranteed job interview. Download the Sector-based work academies leaflet from the DWP website (PDF, 320K) - Opens in a new window.

New Enterprise Allowance

New Enterprise Allowance supports Jobcentre Plus claimants to start their own business. If you can help by providing mentoring support during the early stages of a new business, such as help producing a business plan, please contact your local Jobcentre Plus office - Opens in a new window.

The Work Programme

The Work Programme is the biggest, single, payment by results, employment programme ever introduced. It is delivered by private and voluntary organisations called 'providers'. They are paid to help long-term unemployed people into work.

Work Programme providers are free to innovate and design support that is tailored to an individual's needs. They will work closely with employers in their area to understand and support the local labour market. Your local provider could help you with your recruitment needs and find the right person for the job. Find information about the Work Programme and contact details for your local providers, on the DWP website - Opens in a new window.

Employer pledge

You can get involved in the government's drive to get Britain working by supporting one or more of the above initiatives. Pledge your support to get Britain working.
