IT security

Theft of information from your business

The theft of information can cause your business financial loss, compromise its reputation or even threaten its survival.

You should have a risk management strategy to protect any confidential or personal information your business holds - see our guide to IT risk management.

Dealing with the consequences

If you believe that data has been stolen or your business has been the subject of a scam or fraud, you need to take action to:

  • prevent it continuing
  • discover the extent of the damage
  • clean up the results

The exact course of action will depend on the circumstances. You will need to take specific advice from the police or legal advisors, but the following are typical things to do or consider:

  • Inform the police. The local police may want to involve other units such as the Police Central e-crime Unit (PCeU) of the Metropolitan Police and the e-Crime unit of the Serious Organised Crime Agency (SOCA).
  • Inform your bank and check bank accounts for unexplained transactions.
  • Do a credit check on your business - these are relatively inexpensive and may help to pick up unexpected changes in your business' credit condition.
  • Hire an IT security specialist to investigate whether your IT systems have been compromised and, if necessary, rebuild or replace parts of your IT infrastructure.

Informing people about an information security breach can be an important part of managing the incident. You should be clear about who needs to be notified and why. For example, you should consider notifying:

  • any individuals concerned
  • the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO)
  • relevant regulatory bodies
  • the media
